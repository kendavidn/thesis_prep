Summary
The Institute of Global Health at the University of Geneva proposes a new Certificate of Advanced Studies (CAS) in Health Data Science. This 12-ECTS program provides comprehensive training in R programming, statistical methods, and applied AI tools for public health applications.


The CAS will comprise three courses:


* Introduction to Data Analysis with R for Public Health (12 weeks, 4 ECTS)
* Statistics for Health Research with R (12 weeks, 4 ECTS)
* Applied AI for Health Data (12 weeks, 4 ECTS)


The program will be fully online and part-time, making it accessible to a global audience. Upon completion, participants will be equipped to analyze complex health datasets, create data visualizations, apply statistical methods to public health research, and leverage AI tools for health research productivity.


We envision that the CAS can be launched in the 2025/2026 academic year, with a maximum initial cohort size of 60 students and a target student-to-teacher ratio of 1:15 for optimal learning support.
1. Project Context
Origin of the project:
This CAS is an internal initiative of the Infectious Diseases and Mathematical Modeling (IDMM) division of the Institute of Global Health at the University of Geneva. It builds on the existing expertise of the GRAPH (Global Research and Analyses for Public Health) Courses, a flagship project of the IDMM division. The IDMM division, led by Prof. Olivia Keiser, combines interdisciplinary expertise to advance public health research and education, particularly in infectious diseases.


This CAS program complements the existing MAS (Master of Advanced Studies) in "Santé publique", also led by Prof. Olivia Keiser, which aims to develop experts and leaders in public health. While the MAS focuses on a broad range of public health competencies, the CAS prioritizes technical proficiency in data analysis and AI tools, vital skill sets for modern public health initiatives.
General objectives:
The increasing availability of health data and the complexity of analysis tools implies a growing need for skilled data analysts, as evidenced most recently during the COVID-19 pandemic. To address this gap, our objectives are to:


* Equip health professionals with essential data analysis skills for effective public health policy and response.
* Promote the use of modern and open-source data analysis methods and AI tools in public health contexts.
* Foster innovation in data analysis education by combining online learning, practical projects, and personalized guidance.
2. Relevance & Adequacy of the Planned Training
Market and Societal Needs
The global demand for skilled health data scientists is well-documented and continues to grow. Today, approximately 30% of the world's data volume is being generated by the healthcare industry, and this is expected to increase further[1]. A large proportion of this data is often unused due to insufficient analytical expertise. The World Health Organization (WHO) recommends at least one epidemiological analyst per 200,000 people, a target frequently unmet in many regions[2].


The COVID-19 pandemic underlined the importance of data science for public health. Countries need high-quality data and skilled analysts to understand how diseases spread, track the impact of interventions, and make effective decisions about public health resources. The shortage of personnel trained in data analysis, combined with the massive amount of data being produced by healthcare, points to an urgent need for programs to fill the gap.


Our existing initiative in this domain, the GRAPH Courses, has already demonstrated significant success and demand for this type of training. These courses have benefited over 5,000 learners from more than 50 countries, achieving a 100% satisfaction rate among cohort enrollees. This success serves as a strong foundation and proof of concept for our proposed CAS program.


Building on this momentum, we now seek to expand our offering by delivering a more comprehensive and in-depth training program. The proposed CAS in Health Data Analysis aims to provide a formally accredited, rigorous curriculum that delves deeper into advanced analytical techniques and their application in public health contexts.


Target Audience
The CAS in Health Data Science is designed to cater to a diverse range of professionals and students seeking to enhance their data analysis skills in the context of public health. Our target audience comprises three primary segments:


1. Public Health Professionals (60% of expected participants): This group includes epidemiologists, health policy analysts, program managers, and other professionals working in public health institutions, NGOs, and international organizations. These individuals seek to enhance their data skills to advance their careers and improve their organizations' analytics expertise.


2. Researchers and Recent Graduates in Public Health or Related Fields (30% of expected participants): This segment includes academic researchers, postdoctoral fellows, and recent graduates in public health, epidemiology, biostatistics, and related disciplines. These individuals aim to acquire or refine practical, job-ready skills in health data science to support their research activities or enhance their employability in the field.


3. Data Analysts from Other Sectors (10% of expected participants): This group consists of professionals with strong data analysis backgrounds in fields such as business, finance, or computer science, who aim to transition into the public health domain.
Course Sequence
The CAS in Health Data Science will comprise three courses, totaling 12 ECTS credits. All courses (4 ECTS each) will be delivered on UNIGE's learning management system (Moodle).


The course outlines are summarized below. Full syllabi for all courses are available in the appendix.
1) Introduction to Data Analysis with R for Public Health (12 weeks, 4 ECTS)
Key topics:


* R and RStudio basics
* Visualization with ggplot2
* Data manipulation with tidyverse
* Report creation with R Markdown
* Version control with Git and GitHub
* Interactive dashboards with Quarto
* Practical applications using real public health datasets
2) Statistics for Health Research with R (12 weeks, 4 ECTS)
Key topics:


* Descriptive and inferential statistics
* Linear and logistic regression
* Application of statistical methods to research papers
* Data cleaning and advanced data manipulation
* Geospatial visualization
3) Applied AI for Health Data (12 weeks, 4 ECTS)
Key topics:


* Intro to generative AI and Large language models (LLMs)
* LLM-powered data analysis tools
* Text processing and research synthesis with AI
* Custom AI workflows for health research
* Image and text AI models for health communication and outreach


Learning Modalities
The CAS will employ a blended learning approach, combining self-paced study with interactive workshops and personalized support. The format includes:
1) Pre-work (6 hours per week):
* Video tutorials: Students code along with instructional videos
* Practice exercises: Reinforcing concepts through hands-on application
* Online quizzes: Multiple-choice theory questions and applied data analysis problems
2) Weekly Workshops (2 hours per week):
Interactive sessions where students engage in collaborative data analysis exercises, applying pre-work content and developing new skills while interacting with peers and instructors.
3) Help Sessions (Two 2-hour sessions per week):
Additional support sessions where students receive personalized guidance on the course material, troubleshoot coding issues and discuss project ideas with instructors.
4) Final Projects (14 hours total):
Capstone projects where students apply their acquired skills to significant public health data science topics. These projects will be presented to peers and evaluated by instructors, as a tangible demonstration of students' competencies.

	3. Positioning of the Planned Training
Within UNIGE
This CAS complements existing offerings by focusing specifically on applied data analysis and AI tools for public health, which is a gap in the current offerings. The program also provides a flexible, online format that is accessible to working professionals.


We have reviewed the catalog of courses offered by the Faculty of Medicine and the Computer Science Centre through the Centre for Continuing Education website, confirming that this program fills a unique niche without overlapping existing courses. The Computer Science Center offers generic short courses in French on data visualization, applied data science and Python for data scientists, but there are no existing programs in English and none that combine health data science with R programming and applied AI for Health Data.
International positioning
While similar programs exist, our CAS stands out for:


1. Global accessibility: A fully online format allows participation from anywhere in the world
2. Practical focus: Emphasis on real-world public health data and challenges
3. Cutting-edge content: Integration of the latest data analysis techniques and tools
Pricing
The CAS will be priced at 5,500 CHF, aligning with UNIGE's standard CAS rates.

5. SWOT analysis
The following SWOT analysis provides a comprehensive overview of the proposed CAS program, highlighting its internal strengths and weaknesses, as well as external opportunities and threats.
Strengths
* Strong foundation in existing GRAPH Courses
* The expertise of the IDMM group in global health and disease modeling
* Flexible, online format accessible to international students
* Practical, hands-on approach with real-world applications
Weaknesses
* Limited in-person interaction
* Financial barriers for participants
* Potential technological barriers for some participants
Opportunities
* Growing demand for health data skills globally
* Potential for expansion into other fields requiring data analysis skills
* Strengthening UNIGE's position in data education
Threats
* Competition from other online data science programs
* Rapidly evolving field requiring frequent curriculum updates
* Potential funding challenges for students from LMICs
